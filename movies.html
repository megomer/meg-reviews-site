Lost in Translation(2003)

January 6, 2020

Though flawed, Lost in Translation’s melancholy yet hopeful mood created an atmosphere in which I became completely engrossed. The movie focuses on a washed out actor and his trip to Japan, following his encounters with the culture and the people of such a drastically different culture. Bill Murray portrays the absolute confusion of a man in unfamiliar lands with expressive silence. The culture he observes is depicted almost as if he is on an alien planet. At the start, the way in which his character viewed the people and life of Tokyo almost seemed disdainful, leading me to believe there were subtle racist undertones to the film. However, as the characters became more fleshed out it was easy to see that racism was not the intent of the film. Scarlett Johansson did a wonderful job of playing a woman who feels lost in life. Her subdued desperation made me emphasize highly with her character. Though each actor individually did fantastic, the real focus of the movie was the connection between the two main characters. Where some may see a romance, I see a story of a friendship, deeply connected by their loneliness. The relate to each other, almost seeming like they are the only two sane people in the world. Everyone else in the movie is unable to truly connect with either of them- For Murray’s character it’s the language and culture barrier, with Johansson’s being the differing goals of her and her husband. The age gap may appear strange, especially since Johansson was actually 17 when the movie was filmed, but I think the directors and writers meant for them to be viewed as having an ultimately platonic friendship. Another thing- there wasn't really a story throughout the entire movie, but that may have been the point. To be completely honest, it had a bit too little of a plot for me, and everything that the characters did seemed fruitless. Though sweet, the ending left me with a yearning for more, wishing the characters could continue their carefree lives forever. Lost in Translation offers a low key and somber experience, sure to leave one with a sense of wistful contemplation.

Meg's Rating: 8/10

Star Wars: The Rise of Skywalker(2019)

January 3, 2020

I'm a major Star Wars nerd. I've also never been the gatekeeping type of person that hates on the sequels. In fact, The Force Awakens was the movie that got me into the series in the first place! So I went into this movie with a totally optimistic and excited mindset. It started out rough. Everything felt a bit rushed, and it was a bit difficult to tell what exactly was going on. Honestly, in the first 10 minutes, I kept thinking "Oh, no. Am I going to hate this movie?" However, as it progressed, the pacing issues got ironed out. It ended up being an extremely exhilarating experience. Though it got rid of many of the aspects that The Last Jedi emphasized, it managed to hold on to the few truly meaningful ones. For example, Rey and Kylo’s connection was explored further and with more depth, leading to their relationship easily becoming my favorite part of the trilogy. Rey and Ben Solo were basically all of the high points of the movie. Their characters go through drastic development, but not in a way that seems rushed or unnatural. Rey’s seeming descent into darkness was handled with grace, and Daisy Ridley did a fantastic job depicting her agonizing internal conflict. Adam Driver was phenomenal, especially during touching moments such as his reconnection with his mother. A common complaint about the previous movie was it’s lack of lightsaber fights. Well, The Rise of Skywalker delivers. The battle between Rey and Kylo was emotional, intense, and my now favorite lightsaber duel of the entire saga. Not to mention the set design was beautifully dangerous, adding even more intensity to the fight. I was sitting on the edge of my seat the entire time, and I’m pretty sure I let out an audible gasp at several points during it. As for the ending, I’m quite satisfied. I believe most if not all of the characters had a justified exit. All in all, The Rise of Skywalker wrapped up the saga,especially the trilogy, in an emotional, intense, and satisfying manner.

Meg's Rating: 8.5/10
